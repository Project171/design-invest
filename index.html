Emerging Stronger: Pioneering the New Era of Canadian Commercial Real Estate

Charting a New Course in the Post-Pandemic Economy

In the wake of the global pandemic, Canada's commercial real estate sector stands at a pivotal juncture, poised to play a critical role in shaping the nation's economic resurgence. As a cornerstone of economic growth, this sector not only reflects the health of the broader economy but also drives it forward through innovation, job creation, and investment opportunities. The resilience demonstrated by this sector amidst unprecedented challenges underscores its importance in Canada's journey towards economic recovery and long-term prosperity.

Economic Forces Shaping Commercial Real Estate

Gross Domestic Product (GDP)

  • Direct Correlation: As a broad measure of economic activity, GDP growth typically signals increased business confidence and spending. A rising GDP often translates to higher demand for commercial real estate as companies expand.
  • Investment Attraction: Strong GDP figures can attract domestic and foreign investment in commercial real estate, as investors seek to capitalize on the economic growth.

Consumer Spending

  • Retail Sector Impact: High consumer spending can boost the retail sector, increasing demand for retail spaces in malls and shopping centers.
  • Service Industry Growth: Increased spending also stimulates the service industry, leading to a greater need for office spaces and commercial lots.

Housing Values

  • Wealth Effect: Rising housing values can lead to a 'wealth effect' where homeowners feel financially secure and spend more, benefiting commercial real estate indirectly.
  • Investment Shifts: High residential real estate prices may push investors towards commercial properties, looking for better yields and diversification.

Mortgage Rates

  • Cost of Capital: Lower mortgage rates reduce the cost of borrowing, encouraging investment in real estate development and acquisition.
  • Refinancing Opportunities: For existing property owners, low rates offer refinancing opportunities, freeing up capital for further investment or improvements.

Understanding these relationships is crucial for investors, developers, and businesses when making informed decisions in the commercial real estate space.

VECTOM: The Powerhouses of Canadian Commercial Real Estate

VECTOM: Vancouver, Edmonton, Calgary, Toronto, Ottawa, and Montreal.

The VECTOM markets represent Canada's six largest urban markets with populations over 1 million. These dynamic cities are the economic engines of Canada, leading in terms of GDP contribution, infrastructure development, and demographic growth. Each city within VECTOM showcases unique opportunities and challenges within the commercial real estate sector, making them key areas of focus for investors and businesses alike. Their collective impact on the national economy cannot be overstated, as they continue to drive forward innovation, talent acquisition, and real estate development.

Strategic Investment Insights: Where to Put Your Money in Canada

Buy

Strong Secular Growth Sectors

  • Multifamily: mid-market, modern product not subject to rent control; value-add older vintage with attainable market rents in A locations with the potential to decarbonize
  • Industrial: value-add small and mid bay in "A" locations where land is scarce
  • Grocery-anchored retail: with strong local trade area demographics
  • Cold Storage: modern facilities to support robust population growth
  • Data Centres: in Quebec, Ontario, and BC
  • Student housing: in select markets

Hold

Neutral/Negative Growth Sectors

  • Modern, big box Logistics
  • Trophy, best-in-class Office that meet the amenity and sustainability goals of credit tenants

Sell

Low/Negative Growth Sectors & Regions/Markets

  • Commodity office
  • Enclosed retail
  • Older vintage Industrial: without superior locational attributes
  • Critical evaluation of all assets that are exposed to increasing physical risks and/or decarbonization challenges
